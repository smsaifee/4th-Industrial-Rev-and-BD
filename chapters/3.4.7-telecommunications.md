### 2.3.7 INDUSTRY OF TELECOMMUNICATIONS

This is the most serious time in the history of broadcast communications. Telecom companies have realized that they must go outside their core network earnings in order to be more productive in the face of demographic shifts and the demise of traditional business models. Telcos have multiplied their media holdings, which have turned out to be a substantial source of benefits for telcos as a result of this concentrate on new lines. The barriers between media organizations and telecommunications companies continue to blur. In today's world, telecommunications providers must move beyond traditional services like voice and adapt their most valuable asset-their information-by utilizing media platforms. Big telecoms are currently on the path to transformation and have set out the fundamental methods, establishing media resources, employing existing information resources and purchasing publicizing stages to help construct and promote supported media models sustained. In fact, it's possible that telecoms are the last surviving big information resource that can bridge the financial gap between relevant marketing for consumers and the need to monitor new and consistent revenue sources<sup>[\[41\]](#footnote-41)</sup>.
