## 2.2 WHAT IS THE INDUSTRIAL REVOLUTION?

The process of shifting the agriculture-based economy to a mechanical or instrumental economy is called industrialization, how human society changed their course of living from agriculture to the factory. These innovative changes presented novel approaches to working and living and essentially changed the culture. This interaction started in Britain in the eighteenth century and spread to different regions. The term Industrial Revolution was first promoted by the English economics student of history Arnold Toynbee (1852-83) to portray Britain's financial improvement from 1760 to 1840<sup>[\[1\]](#footnote-1)</sup>.
